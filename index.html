Time Management
Your Daily Plan
Structured by Role
Your day is not left to improvisation. Each role has a structured sequence — follow it and you'll hit the number.
JR Chaser — Daily Structure
1 · Morning Review
Open all leads. Who needs follow-up today? Who's hitting Day 8 tomorrow? Prioritize before calling.
2 · Calls & Faxes
Start with oldest leads first (Day 6+). Each active lead gets a focused call. Log result immediately after each call.
3 · TL Check-In
Mandatory daily session. Present every stuck lead. TL listens to 2-4 calls per chaser per day. Mistakes caught early.
4 · Log & End-of-Day Plan
All calls logged: date, result, next step. Check who hits Warning tomorrow. Come in ready — not starting from zero.
Chaser — Daily Structure
1 · Priority Sort
Who's Day 7+? Who needs a second fax? Who is approaching Warning? Sort by urgency before starting.
2 · Follow-Up Calls
~3.4 active leads per day (75 ÷ 22 days). Give each lead focused, quality time. Not just a call — a result.
3 · New Lead Faxes
Day 1-3 leads: call + fax + log on the same day. Never push to tomorrow. Aging starts from day one.
4 · Log & Close-Day Plan
Update every lead: result, next follow-up date, current status. Check who hits Warning and any incoming DOs.
SR Chaser — Daily Structure
1 · Hard Lead Review
Focus on Day 10+ leads. Try a different doctor or department. Flag anything needing a management decision.
2 · High-Priority Calls
Harder leads take longer per call. SR's higher conv% comes from persistence and strategy — not speed.
3 · JR Support
Help JR with stuck leads — guidance, not doing it for them. "Try medical records instead of front desk."
4 · Log & TL Report
Log with high precision. Report to TL any lead needing a decision above your authority level.

Chasing Workflow — 45-Day Cycle
How a Lead Moves
Through the 45-Day Cycle
STEP 01Lead Assignment — Lead assigned in sheet, timestamp recorded, visible in chaser queue. Source hidden from chaser — management tracks separately.
STEP 02First Touch within 24h. Call doctor's office, confirm mutuality, get fax number, identify right contact. Max tolerated delay: 48h. After 48h = red flag, immediate action.
STEP 03Fax sent same day if mutuality confirmed. Timestamp logged. Any delay must have a reason logged. Confirm receipt and expected handling time.
STEP 04Follow-up cadence: FU1 next business day → FU2 48h later → FU3 72h later. 48h grace period between each follow-up after fax sent.
STEP 05Obtain DO + Chart Notes. DO ≤ 2 months old · CN ≤ 5 months 10 days. Both required. Update sheet status at every step: Assigned → First Touch Done → Mutuality Confirmed → Fax Sent → Receipt Confirmed → Follow-Up Ongoing → RX Received.
RESULTSigned RX submitted → backend → client review → Medicare billing → revenue. This is the only output that counts.
45-Day Cycle — 3 Review Gates
Day 14EARLY SIGNAL REVIEW
Not final judgment — detect early warning signs only. Review: first-touch compliance · no-movement cases · fax speed · early team movement · TL control quality. Idle leads transferred to SR if no progress.
Day 30CORRECTIVE REVIEW
Trend improvement · progress toward RX% · weak performer movement · TL coaching impact. Dashboard check: what moved, what stayed weak, what insights. Leads still alive — no forced close at Day 30.
Day 45FINAL REVIEW
Final conversion by chaser + TL · source performance · reward decisions · PIP decisions · exit decisions · source scale/reduce decisions. Cycle officially closes. Exception: TL may approve lead retention if genuine potential remains.
Source rule: Chaser sees one queue only. Management analyzes by source in background. Source scale/reduce decisions made every 45 days. Outsourced leads quality-gated before entering chasing.

Quality Indicator
Are They Working Hard
or Working Smart?
Two conversion rates tell two different stories. Read both to know exactly where someone stands.
Rate 1 — Lead Conversion
DOs ÷ Leads Assigned × 100
Measures how many leads turned into actual Doctor Orders. This is the output metric — the only one that generates revenue. Every agent's primary score.
Rate 2 — Action Quality
DOs ÷ Total Actions × 100
Measures how many actions it took to produce each DO. This is the efficiency metric — it tells you the quality of their work, not just the quantity.
Supporting Execution KPIs
· First Touch Compliance % — touched within 24h
· Same-Day Fax Compliance %
· No-Movement Leads — 3+ days no update
· Overdue Follow-Ups
· Office Reach Rate
· Fax Sent Rate
How to Read the Combination
High Lead Conv% + High Action Conv%
Working smart and effectively. Getting DOs from leads efficiently. This is Elite performance — reinforce and keep.
High Lead Conv% + Low Action Conv%
Getting results but using too many actions. Good output, needs coaching on efficiency — may be over-calling or logging duplicates.
Low Lead Conv% + High Action Conv%
Working smart on every action but not converting leads. Likely a lead quality issue or wrong doctor selection. Coach on lead strategy — not effort.
Low Lead Conv% + Low Action Conv%
Not converting leads AND not making quality actions. Real warning signal — no output and no real effort. Escalate immediately: warning + coaching + close monitoring.

The chaser's primary responsibility is to return signed RXs through disciplined and consistent follow-up execution.

Consequences System
Level 1Verbal Warning — missed first-touch, repeated late follow-up, weak daily execution.
Level 2Written Warning — same issue repeated in same week, no-movement from negligence.
Level 314-Day PIP — low performance vs minimum, no improvement after coaching.
Level 4Final Review / Exit — no improvement after PIP, repeated lead waste, clear mismatch.
Fixed Operating Rules
Cycle: Day 1–45 official. Day 14 = Early Signal · Day 30 = Corrective · Day 45 = Final
Lead Load: 75 new leads per chaser per 30-day window
First Touch: Required within 24h · Max 48h (overload exception) · After 48h = red flag
Source Visibility: Chaser does NOT see source. Management tracks internally. Source decisions every 45 days.
Outsourced Leads: Internal quality gate required before entering chasing queue.
